Instructions

In this experiment, we are interested in studying how people learn. Your task will be to study 60 pictures of unusual objects on a series of slides. You should try to memorize as many of the objects as you can. Directly after the study period, you will be tested on your memory.

It is often hard to learn when information is presented too fast or twoo slow. In this experiment, you will be asked to adjust how long a pause you want between each picture. We are interested knowing the rate of information people prefer. Please choose a speed that you believe will best allow you to remember 60 objects. If you choose a fast pace, you will not finish the HIT any earlier than choosing a slower pace as there is a task at the end to fill the remaining time.

To reiterate, we are interested in how well memory works when people can control the rate of information presentation. Please do not write anything down or use screen recordings. Just use your mind and intelligence!
