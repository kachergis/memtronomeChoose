Instructions

In this experiment, you will study pictures of unusual objects on a series of slides. Your goal is to try to remember 60 pictures you will see, one at a time. You will get to choose how long a pause there is between each picture, because we are interested in seeing what rate of information people prefer, similar to seeing if people prefer a lecturer speaking faster or slower.

Please choose a speed that will best allow you to remember 60 objects. Note that if you choose a fast pace, you will not finish any earlier than choosing a slower pace: we will give you a short task at the end to fill the remaining time.

Directly after the study period, you will be asked which images you remember from study.

To reiterate, we are interested in how well memory works when people can control the rate of information presentation. Please do not write anything down or use screen recordings. Just use your mind and intelligence!
